# Lovecraft Design Reference

## Overview
Lovecraft is a digital sanctuary for romantic storytelling. The design language balances "High-End Agency" aesthetics with "Intimate Editorial" warmth.

## Core Principles
- **Intimacy through Typography:** High-contrast pairings (Serif for emotion, Sans-Serif for structure).
- **Cinematic Motion:** Using GSAP for storytelling reveals rather than just decoration.
- **Soft Materiality:** Glassmorphism, subtle radial washes, and grainy textures that feel "organic."
- **Zero-Clutter Philosophy:** Ruthless removal of "AI Tells" (no em-dashes, no meta-labels like "Section 01").

## Visual Tokens
- **Palette:** 
  - `Rose Blush`: Soft paper-like background.
  - `Rose Deep`: High-contrast near-black with a warm red tint.
  - `Rose Medium`: The primary brand accent.
- **Typography:**
  - **Headlines:** `Cormorant Garamond` (Italic, Light/Medium).
  - **Body/UI:** `Outfit` (Modern, clean, premium geometric).
  - **Data/Meta:** `Geist Mono` (Precision).
- **Layout:** Asymmetric grids, overlapping elements, and wide-container heroes.

## Interactive Patterns
- **Scrubbing Text Reveal:** Paragraphs that light up word-by-word on scroll.
- **Image Scale & Fade:** Visuals that "breathe" as they enter the viewport.
- **Magnetic Physics:** Micro-interactions that pull toward the cursor.

## Prohibited Patterns (The "Anti-Slop" Guardrails)
- **No Em-Dashes (`—`):** Strictly use commas or periods.
- **No Meta-Labels:** Never label sections with "Section 01" or "Step 04."
- **No Inter Default:** Always use Outfit or Geist for a premium feel.
- **No Centered Wall of Text:** Maintain wide containers (max 2-3 lines for H1).
